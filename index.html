DexQBU: High-Efficient Reinforcement Learning for Dexterous Manipulation

Kexin ZHENG1, Yu Luo Liu2, Lei Lv3, Kai Chen1, Qi Dou1

1Department of Computer Science and Engineering, Chinese University of Hong Kong,
2 Huawei Noah's Ark Lab,
3Shanghai Research Institute for Intelligent Autonomous Systems, Tongji University

Abstract

Dexterous manipulation tasks, characterized by complex 3D observations and high degrees of freedom, pose significant challenges for reinforcement learning (RL), especially when considering the inherent demand for high-quality data and efficient exploration. To address these challenges, we propose \textbf{DexQBU}, a unified framework that combines upstream representation learning with downstream policy optimization in a synergistic architecture. In the upstream phase, we leverage PointNet for domain-specific segmentation of 3D point clouds to extract local geometric features, while ULIP-2 aligns these features within a semantic space enriched by a 3D foundational model. This hierarchical processing bridges the gap between raw sensory input and actionable policy learning. For downstream optimization, we introduce the Quantile-Boosted Actor-Critic (QBAC) algorithm, a novel off-policy method that mitigates regression toward low-quality data by prioritizing high-quantile-based updates. By integrating global feature dimensionality reduction upstream with a high-exploited optimized policy downstream, DexQBU narrows the exploration space and maximizes information extraction from limited data. Evaluated on the DexArt benchmark, DexQBU achieves high success rates while reducing required environmental interactions by \textbf{3.9$\times$} compared to prior methods, which demonstrates the efficacy of unifying representation learning with data-efficient RL, offering a pathway for scalable dexterous manipulation in real-world applications.
